31. Raw data and background rates    Appendix

In this appendix we find a few more figures and numbers about the background rates and a comparison to the raw data rates. Fig. 1 shows the raw data rate of the full center chip of the entire CAST data taking campaign in the same units as used for the normal background rate plots. The background increases towards low energies, but a hump is visible in the \(\SIrange{2}{3}{keV}\) range, matching the expected energies for muon tracks. The rates are roughly four orders of magnitude larger than the best achieved background rates.

Further, in sec. 31.1 we find background rates not only for the \goldArea region, but for the entire center chip. Both to compare with fig. 1 as well as to get a better idea of the efficiency of the vetoes.

31.1. Background rates over full chip

Fig. 2 shows the effect of the different vetoes over the entire chip using the \lnL method at \(\SI{80}{\%}\) software efficiency as a base. It better highlights the efficiency of some vetoes, for example the FADC veto is more effective over the entire chip, than in the center \goldArea region alone (compare with fig. 71). Of course, the septem and line vetoes are even more efficient over the entire chip, as expected and previously seen in the form of the background suppression plots (for example fig. #fig:background:background_suppression_comparison).

Fig. 3 shows how the different software efficiencies affect the background rate comparing different \lnL and MLP efficiencies including all vetoes.

Finally, tab. 30 shows the mean background rates over the entire chip for all considered \lnL and MLP veto setups in the energy range \(\SIrange{0.2}{8}{keV}\). It is the equivalent of tab. 24 for the full chip.
